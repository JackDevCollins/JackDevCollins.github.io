 PROJECT NAME 

Dragon Ninja Clan: Sword 

of Destiny

— ROLE

Level Design & Environmental Art



— DATE

2019

Working on Dragon Ninja Clan: Sword of Destiny is an experience I will never forget. A man who shall remain nameless hired Richland College to create a “proof of concept” of a game for a series he had been working on for several years. Our class of about 20 or so were tasked with making a game following the direction of this man. The 3D side scrolling beat 'em up we produced was an overall success that we were all proud of. After the project we were made to sign some documents and delete any files or backups we had of the project. After almost a year and a half of hearing nothing we got word that the game was added to Steam. The levels I worked on had minor changes other than custom decals of the company’s website and logo splattered on every surface imaginable. Normally I would not be so upfront about the development difficulties but after doing some research I found that the director was arrested for drug possession with intention to sell so I’m not too worried about legal ramifications. Despite the troubles this is still the first commercially available game I have worked on and am proud of what my team and I were able to accomplish.
